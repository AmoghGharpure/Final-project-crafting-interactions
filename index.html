Plantzee

A platform to communicate with your daily potted plants as part of your family

Project Background

Acknowledging plants as beings of tranquility and healing for those in distress, we wound our case study around potted plants nurturing in a home environment.

My Role

  • Plan user research activities to understand the needs and behaviors of the target audience
  • Analyze and synthesize the data to identify patterns and insights that inform the design of the app
  • Conduct user testing to evaluate the usability of the app and identify areas for improvement.

Summary

Plantzee provides relevant and accessible information of potted plants to the user in a friendly gamified manner to increase the engagement and increase awareness

Understanding

The goal was to develop a deep understanding of the challenge, and to identify opportunities for innovation that can inform the next phase of design

Design Process

We worked on the standard double diamond approach to tackle this problem area. As plants are part of nature and not exactly a machine designed by humans, it was essential to understand in depth the effect of plants on human behavior, factors affecting plant health and the most important part of communication ways with a plant.

Initial Design Challenge

" How might we develop a user-friendly platform that provides relevant and accessible information for casual plant growers and beginners to help them understand and care for their plants? "

Area identification

With thousands of layoffs and pandemic, people were forced to spend time indoors and in their concrete structures. An environment of stress and self-doubt was spreading. With plants having an inherent soothing factor in addition to being a good companion, we narrowed down on design something sustainable, useful and people-friendly around the area of plants.

Desk Research

Collected statistics and figures from sources and studies about how much plants impact our daily lives and what scope do we have to design further.

Interview insights

Mapping the information from the interviews brought us down to 3 categories mainly the purpose, information and concern around plants.

TAkeaways

Affinity mapping and identifying patterns resulted in the following 4 takeaways from the entire research process.

Defining

The next step after understanding and research was the defining phase which mainly involved focusing on the scope of project and laying out elements around it.

Persona

Based on the 4 interviews, we created an ideal persona and a background story to fit in our design.

User journey

The user journey provided a basis for making informed decisions about design and development priorities.

Key points to consider for the design stage

As we followed a user-centric approach of persona, user journey and scenario, it was helpful to identify the top 4 features to be included in the design.

Design

With a better understanding of what we are designing for, we dived into ideation phase with some hand-sketches.

Low-fidelity wireframes

3 task flows were identified based on the features of the app-

- Identifying a plant
- Adding a plant to personal set
- Maintaining the added plant with the help of the app

We tested the concepts and validated the steps with the help of brainstorming and inviting a few of the past users to comment.

MId-fidelity wireframes

Post laying out the idea of the main features and flow, we dived into positioning the elements into a digital mockup. This made the picture more clear of what additional elements are required to support the page and what can be decluttered.

HIgh-fidelity wireframes

For giving more importance to the images of the plants, we resorted to dark UI and a subtle color scheme.


1. Flow 1: Logging in for the first time and exploring the homepage

2. Flow 2: Exploring and adding a plant to My Plants list and understanding the features

3. Flow 3: Maintaining and monitoring the added plant health using the app

User testing

We carried out 3 User tests in person and analyzed the feedback to form the next set of action.

Prototype

Post the changes and additions as per User feedback, we created a full-fledged prototype on Protopie software

Project Learnings

  • Crucial nature of user research : Gaining valuable insights about the target audience, their needs, pain points, and expectations from the app
  • Intuitive design and clear navigation: Designing for a beginner audience meant keeping the user experience simple and easy-to-use
  • Keeping the design visually appealing: As the app was related to plants, color association while retaining the freshness of visual system was important
  • Providing relevant & factually correct information: Care requirements, growth habits, and any potential issues that could arise
  • Using clear & simple language: Avoiding any technical jargon that a beginner may not understand
  • Continuous iteration & testing: Ensuring that the app was meeting user needs and expectations
